__Notes on raising COMMQ tickets__

- COMMQ tickets can be raised by the PM/CSE as soon as a project is known about, to give the Commissioning Team (DMH) early visibility.
- The raising PM/CSE should include notes specifying the expected date commissioning can start, and the desired completion date.
- The raiser should assign the ticket to DMH ONLY once the prerequisites below have been met.


__Pre-requisites (to be performed by PM)__

- Clone this ticket and modify the customer name, owner, and internal owner field on the new cloned ticket.
**** I REPEAT: CLONE THIS TICKET NOW. Do not edit this ticket or our template will get screwed up. ****

- Update the summary line to remove the word Template, leaving the COMMQ: in the summary.

- Set the "Customer keywords" field of this ticket to be the due date (if any) in the form MMDDYYYY.

- Update the Customer Contact information below.

- Update the Access Equipment Info below to say what kinds of equipment we need to set up for test lines.

- Set the internal owner to DMH to place this ticket on the queue.



__Customer Contact__
Eric Huffman

__Access Equipment Information__
CMTS / Cable MTA



__Instructions for CE__

Check in DQP4 for the range of test lines. If missing please contact customer and request test DN needed.

1. Review the access equipment information above.
2. Make sure you know how to build the access devices / test lines (using MetaView Web if possible).
3. Call the customer contact and walk them through building these test lines. Try to have the customer do it all themselves with instructions from you.
4. Once the test lines have been built, walk the customer through modifying the call services that are enabled on each line. By the end of this exercise you want all the test lines to have all the required call services enabled (so that they're ready for testing).
5. Follow-up your phone call by sending an email to the customer reviewing what you did, and how you did it (so that they can refer to this email later if they forget). The email doesn't need to be massively detailed - just enough to get them started.
6. Ensure that all L3 Notices are complete as a final step to prepare the site for handover.

Once the test lines have been confiured, please set the "With whom" field to "customer" and KEEP the ticket until the customer as completed the acceptance testing (typically 2-3 weeks)

In an effort to help train the customers the CE should do the following:
-If customer hits a problem (maybe in translations, or with a call service), get them to log in to SAS and look
at the problem with you. The next time they hit a problem, get them to first have a look at SAS. Get the
customer into the habit of looking in SAS first before they call for help.
-Get the customer to modify the subscriber settings (in MVWeb) as they try out different features.
-When handing a customer over to the support team, we need to include some information about how
skilled the customer is or is not with our equipment (maybe saying what they have or haven’t done).

Once the customer has confirmed they are ready to accept the testing, please set
the "Resolution" field of this issue to "Task Done";
the "with whom" field to "Customer";
the "Customer ticket status" to "Resolved"
and reassign this task back to the PM, who will close.
